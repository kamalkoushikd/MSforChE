🔬 General Properties

Atomic Number: 35

Atomic Mass: 79.904 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s² 4p⁵

Appearance: Reddish-brown liquid with a pungent odor

🏗 Structural Properties

Crystal Structure: Orthorhombic (solid state at low temperatures)

Lattice Parameters:

  • a: 6.67 Å
  • b: 4.47 Å
  • c: 8.72 Å

Density: Liquid: 3.102 g/cm³, Solid: 4.05 g/cm³

🛠 Mechanical Properties

Mohs Hardness: Not applicable (liquid state at room temperature)

Tensile Strength: Low (brittle when solid)

Elastic Modulus: Not well-defined for liquid bromine

💎 Optical Properties

Refractive Index: ~1.001 (liquid bromine at 20°C)

Color & Transparency: Deep reddish-brown liquid, transparent in thin layers

Photoluminiscence: Weak in pure form

Absorption and Emission spectra: Strong absorption in the uv and blue light regions

🔥 Chemical Properties

Common Oxidation States: -1, +1, +3, +5, +7

Electronegativity: 2.96 (Pauling scale)

Ionization Energy:

  • First: 1139.9 kJ/mol Second: 2103 kJ/mol

Reactivity:

  • With Oxygen: Forms bromine oxides (Br₂O, BrO₂, Br₂O₅)
  • With Water: Partially soluble, forming hypobromous acid (HBrO) and hydrobromic acid (HBr)
  • With Acids: Reacts with sulfuric acid to form Br₂ gas

Most Common Compounds:

  • Hydrogen bromide (HBr)
  • Sodium bromide (NaBr)
  • Bromine trifluoride (BrF₃)
  • Bromine chloride (BrCl)

🌡 Physical Properties

Melting Point: -7.2°C

Boiling Point: 58.8°C

Thermal Conductivity: 0.12 W/m·K (liquid state)

Specific Heat Capacity: 0.473 J/g·K (liquid)

Electrical Conductivity: Poor (non-metal, does not conduct electricity)
