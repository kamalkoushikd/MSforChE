🔬 General Properties

Atomic Number ⚛️

The atomic number of an element is the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom. It is unique for each element and is used to identify an element.

Atomic Mass 🧲

The atomic number represents the number of protons, while atomic mass is the weighted average of isotopes. These define the element's identity and stability. It is measured in Atomic Mass Unit (amu).

Electronic Configuration 🌟

Describes the arrangement of electrons in orbitals, helps determining chemical properties and reactivity 🔥.

🔨 Structural Properties

Crystal Structure 🌌

Describes the arrangement of atoms in a crystalline solid. It is determined by the arrangement of atoms, ions, or molecules in a regular repeating pattern.

{% include "includes/lattice.html" %}

Lattice Constants 📏

Describes the size of the unit cell in a crystal lattice. It is the distance between the lattice points in a crystal structure.

target="_blank"Density ⚖️

Describes the mass of a substance per unit volume. It is measured in g/cm³ or kg/m³.

Packing Factor 🗃️

Packing factor indicates how tightly atoms are packed. These affect material weight and mechanical properties

🔧 Mechanical Properties

Hardness 💎

Measures resistance to deformation or scratching. Important for wear resistance in materials.

  • Mohs Scale 📏

    • The Mohs scale of hardness ranks minerals from 1 (talc, softest) to 10 (diamond, hardest) based on which can scratch others. Created by Friedrich Mohs in 1812, it's used by geologists for mineral identification, though the scale is non-linear.

Tensile Strength 🪢

Maximum stress a material can withstand while being stretched. Crucial for structural applications.

Ductility & Brittleness 🧵

Ductility is the ability to deform plastically; brittleness is the tendency to break without deformation. These determine material flexibility.

Elastic Modulus 📐

Indicates stiffness by measuring stress-strain response. Essential for load-bearing applications.

🔮 Optical Properties

Color & Transparency 🎨

Determines how light interacts with the material (e.g., absorption, reflection). Useful in aesthetics and optics.

Refractive Index 🔍

Measures how light bends when passing through a material. Critical for lenses and optical devices.

Photoluminescence 💡

Emission of light after absorbing photons. Used in LEDs and sensors when applicable.

Absorption & Emission Spectra 📊

Characterizes energy levels and transitions in atoms or molecules, aiding in spectroscopy applications.

🔥 Chemical Properties

Common Oxidation States ⚗️

Indicates typical charges during reactions, crucial for predicting chemical behavior.

Reactivity with Oxygen, Water, and Acids ⚛️

Defines how elements interact with common substances, important for corrosion resistance and reactions.

Electronegativity & Ionization Energy ⚡

Electronegativity shows electron affinity; ionization energy indicates energy needed to remove an electron. Both predict bonding behavior.

Most Common Compounds 🧪

Examples like H2O or CO2 highlight practical uses and natural occurrences of elements.

🧊 Physical Properties

Melting & Boiling Points 🌡️

Indicate phase change temperatures, critical for material applications.

Thermal Conductivity 🌡️

Measures heat transfer efficiency, vital for insulation or heat dissipation materials.

Specific Heat Capacity 🌡️

Energy required to raise temperature per unit mass; important for thermal management.

Thermal Expansion 🌡️

Indicates how well a material expands or contracts with temperature changes.
